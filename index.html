Why India Struggles with Civic Sense?

Lack of civic sense is not a genetic trait; it is a complex social and systemic issue.

Casteism & Classism

For centuries, sanitation work was tied to specific communities, creating a mental block where cleaning is seen as "someone else's job." This mentality reduces personal responsibility.

FACT: Over 90% of sanitation workers belong to Dalit communities.

Weak Government Systems

If the environment is dirty and infrastructure like bins or toilets are missing, people follow a "herd mentality" and litter more. Discipline requires supportive systems.

NOTE: Infrastructure is the foundation of civic discipline.

How India Can Improve

1. Education

Schools should teach "Osoji" (self-cleaning) to build respect for labor and responsibility from childhood.

2. Systems

Better urban planning, clean toilets, and frequent garbage collection points encourage good behavior.

3. Enforcement

Strict fines and high probability of punishment (CCTV monitoring) create a culture of discipline.

4. Empathy

Promoting the concept of 'Samfundssind' (social-mindedness) ‚Äî putting society's interest above personal comfort.
